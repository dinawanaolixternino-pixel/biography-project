I Wanted to Save Lives, Now I Just Save Files

Childhood Years

1 I grew up in a quiet province called Mahayag in Zamboanga del Sur. 2 My parents were away working, so I lived with my Lolo and Lola in their simple home. 3 Life there was very peaceful, but honestly, it was quite lonely sometimes. 4 Our house was situated on a large farm, and the neighbors were really far away from us. 5 Because of the vast distance, it was rare to see other kids my age outside playing. 6 Most of the time, my only playmates were the stray cats and dogs in our yard. 7 The only time our house felt truly lively was when my cousins came to visit us. 8 One time, I even threw my cousin's slippers away because I didn't want them to go home. 9 I cried so hard that time because I knew that I would get lonely again once they left. 10 But because I was mostly alone, I eventually learned to love the small things around me. 11 I loved sitting on the porch just watching the chickens eat their food in the dirt. 12 Feeding the pigs became a daily routine that I actually looked forward to every morning. 13 My favorite bonding moment was going fishing with my Lolo at the nearby river. 14 Sometimes we caught a fish for dinner, and sometimes we went home with nothing. 15 I also remember a specific season when many butterflies would suddenly appear inside our house. 16 It was such a beautiful sight since there were many butterflies with different bright colors. 17 When it was almost nighttime, my grandfather and I would walk back home together slowly. 18 As we approached, I could smell the delicious food my Lola was cooking for us. 19 We would eat together by the light of a lamp, telling stories about our day. 20 It was sad not having playmates, but looking back, my childhood was truly beautiful.

Teenage Years

1 When I was a teenager, high school was full of fun, awkward moments, and unforgettable memories. 2 I remember back in Grade 8, I joined a school pageant just to be exempted from subjects. 3 Walking on that stage was terrifying, and I honestly regretted signing up immediately. 4 My knees were shaking the whole time, and I felt so shy in front of the crowd. 5 However, it remains a fun memory because it was my first time trying something so brave. 6 High school was very different from the quiet life I had in the province. 7 I made new friends who were loud, crazy, and always ready for fun adventures. 8 We loved hanging out at the canteen during recess just to talk about random things. 9 After school, my friends and I often went to Lemon Square to buy food. 10 We would also play mobile games and scream in excitement whenever we won a tough match. 11 I also remember that I really liked my Science and Math teacher back then. 12 I would always raise my hand during class just to catch her attention. 13 I even studied so hard just so I could answer many of her questions correctly. 14 It makes me laugh now thinking about the effort I made just to impress her. 15 Then, the COVID-19 pandemic suddenly arrived and changed everything for all of us. 16 It was really sad because I wasnâ€™t able to enjoy the rest of my high school life. 17 We were stuck at home and missed out on many events we were looking forward to. 18 The graduation ceremony we dreamed of never happened in the way we expected. 19 Despite the abrupt ending, I am still grateful for the short time we had together. 20 High school was incomplete, but the memories we made before the lockdown are forever.

College Years

1 Stepping into college was a confusing time because my life plans suddenly changed. 2 I actually wanted to study Nursing to help take care of sick people. 3 But I only applied to two schools, which were PLM and UE, hoping for the best. 4 Sadly, there were no slots left for nursing at PLM, which really broke my heart. 5 Since I had limited options, I decided to enroll at UE in Caloocan instead. 6 I ended up taking Computer Science, a path I never really expected to walk on. 7 I quickly realized that this course was much harder than I imagined it would be. 8 Programming is not just about typing; it requires deep logic and a lot of patience. 9 I remember feeling happy when I wrote my first code, but that was just the start. 10 There are days when I stare at my screen for hours finding a missing semicolon. 11 It is very frustrating when the program has errors and I cannot fix them immediately. 12 I often stay up very late at night just to finish my complex coding assignments. 13 Sometimes I forget to eat or sleep because I am too focused on the monitor. 14 Thankfully, I met new friends who are also struggling with the same hard subjects. 15 We help each other debug code and study for the difficult major exams. 16 Aside from coding, I also enjoy learning how to design layouts and create wireframes. 17 There are moments when I honestly ask myself if this course is right for me. 18 But every time I finally solve a bug, I feel a huge rush of excitement. 19 I am learning to accept this new path and not give up on challenges. 20 Although I didn't become a nurse, I am excited to become a successful developer now.
